Huiyu LI


Hello! I am actively seeking an exciting and challenging postdoctoral position.

I received my Ph.D. from INRIA in Sophia Antipolis, France, under the guidance of Prof. Hervé Delingette and Prof. Nicholas Ayache. I was also fortunate to receive support from the French government through the 3IA Côte d’Azur investments program. Previously, I completed a master's degree in Computer Science at Beijing Institute of Technology.


The goal of my research is to empower AI to advance healthcare. I am particularly interested in applying generative models to medical image anonymization, enabling the removal of patient-identifiable information while preserving diagnostic information. This challenging task involves achieving a delicate balance between image reconstruction and anonymization, as well as identity removal and utility preservation. Outside of my academic pursuits, I enjoy writing, gardening, skiing, and playing badmiminton.


Dear Like-minded Friends, If something I share resonates with you or touches your heart, I'd love to hear from you! Feel free to reach out via email or schedule a time to chat through Chatting Time. Let's connect and see the world through each other's eyes. I've always believed that everyone is like a book. My pages are filled with fashion design, architecture, interior decoration, gardening, cooking, drawing, and dreaming. I look forward to sharing and learning together!

STORIES

    Beyond my research, I am a writer. I write not for sale, but for my soul—especially when I cannot find a book that truly entertains it.

    As the saying goes, words come from life. In that sense, I see myself as a “generator,” learning from real-life experiences and transforming them into written words. I am not a very good liar, nor am I a model that can easily “unlearn”. My words inevitably reflect my personality and my life, making it easy to trace who I am. For this reason, much of my writing may never be publicly published while I am alive. However, I am always happy to share the core thoughts and inspirations behind my words.

    I have always believed that everyone is like a book, worth reading and appreciating.
